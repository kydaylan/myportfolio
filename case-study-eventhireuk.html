The Brief

eventhireuk.com is the flagship website for eventhireGroup, a market-leading UK events hire company operating across four nationwide depots. The site serves both B2B and B2C customers, covering everything from furniture hire and catering equipment to refrigeration and event accessories.

When I took ownership of the digital strategy, the site was managed by an external agency. The business needed full in-house control, a modern rebuild, and a long-term SEO and content strategy to drive commercial growth.

The Challenge

This was not a simple redesign. The project spanned four major workstreams, each with its own complexity.

Full Site Build

Built a brand new 3,000+ page WordPress website from scratch, covering hundreds of product categories, location pages, landing pages, and blog content.

Live Migration

Migrated from the agency-built site to the new build without losing traffic, rankings, or commercial functionality.

SEO Recovery

Recovered from SEO sabotage after the outgoing agency de-indexed key commercial pages via Google Search Console during the handover.

Data-Driven Strategy

Used analytics data to make the case for high-value location pages that stakeholders initially questioned. These pages now drive a third of all traffic.

What I Built

Full WordPress website covering:

  • Product catalogue with hundreds of items across multiple categories
  • Location pages targeting key UK cities and regions
  • Blog and content hub for SEO and informational queries
  • Custom landing pages for commercial campaigns
  • Integration with the business's existing booking and enquiry workflows

Technical infrastructure:

  • Plesk server management with staging and live deployment workflows
  • Cloudflare CDN configuration, caching rules, and security setup
  • Core Web Vitals optimisation — desktop PageSpeed scores improved from 50+ to 90+
  • Structured data implementation (FAQ, Product, LocalBusiness, and custom service schema) across the site using ACF field structures

The SEO Recovery

After the migration, I discovered that the previous agency had de-indexed several key commercial pages through Google Search Console. High-value pages targeting terms like "furniture hire" and "chair hire" had been removed from Google's index entirely.

Identified affected pages

Audited Search Console to find all de-indexed commercial pages

Re-submitted for indexing

Submitted all affected URLs and verified crawlability

Structured data implementation

Implemented FAQ, Product, LocalBusiness, and custom service schema across the site

Sustained SEO strategy

Built ongoing content and optimisation programme targeting commercial keywords

Rankings restored

First-page positions recovered over 12+ months of consistent work

The Location Pages Decision

Location pages now drive approximately one-third of the site's total traffic. These pages target searches like "furniture hire London", "catering equipment hire Manchester", and similar location-specific commercial queries.

These pages almost didn't exist. I used Google Analytics and Search Console data to demonstrate the search demand and commercial intent behind location-based queries, making the case that these pages were essential for organic growth. The data proved the value, and the pages have since become one of the site's strongest traffic drivers.

What I Learned

A 3,000+ page site build under time pressure requires constant prioritisation. Not every page gets the attention it deserves on the first pass, and that's a trade-off you have to accept when balancing speed with quality. The key is building a foundation that allows you to iterate and improve over time, which is exactly what happened here — the site continues to grow and improve with each quarter.

The SEO sabotage taught me the importance of owning every access point. I now ensure that all Google Search Console, Analytics, and DNS credentials are held in-house from day one on every project.

The location pages decision reinforced something I come back to constantly: let the data make the argument. When stakeholders question a content strategy, real traffic and revenue data is the most effective way to demonstrate value.
