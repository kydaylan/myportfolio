The Brief

In 2022, eventhireGroup — a collection of event hire companies operating across multiple sectors (furniture, catering equipment, refrigeration, reusable cups, exhibitions) — needed a cohesive brand identity. Each company had evolved independently, resulting in inconsistent visual identities that didn't reflect the group's position as a market leader.

The Challenge

Unifying multiple established brands under one cohesive identity — without losing what made each one work in its own market.

Legacy Branding

Multiple companies under one group, each with its own legacy branding and visual identity.

No Brand Cohesion

No consistent brand language across the group — each company looked unrelated.

Sector Diversity

Each brand serves different market sectors (furniture, catering, refrigeration, cups, exhibitions) so needed individual identity while feeling connected.

Touchpoint Coverage

All customer touchpoints needed updating: websites, print materials, vehicle livery, signage, uniforms.

Brand Recognition

Had to maintain brand recognition for established brands while modernising the overall identity.

What I Did

Led the complete rebrand of all eventhireGroup companies:

  • Designed new logos for each company within a unified design system
  • Created comprehensive brand guidelines covering typography, colour palettes, logo usage, and visual language
  • Ensured each brand maintained its sector-specific identity while being clearly part of the eventhireGroup family
  • Updated all 9 websites to reflect the new branding
  • Coordinated rollout across all customer touchpoints: digital, print, signage, vehicle livery
  • Worked with suppliers and print partners to ensure consistent reproduction across all media

Design Approach

The challenge was balancing unity with individuality. Each company needed to feel like part of a family while being distinct enough to serve its own market.

The core "eventhire" brands share a split-weight wordmark: "event" in bold white, "hire" in grey, with the sector name (UK, Refrigeration) positioned beneath. Each brand gets its own colour: blue for the flagship, teal for refrigeration, red for Spaceworks, sky blue for Blue Sky. Event Cup Solutions and Eventex have distinct identities with custom icons (globe, diamond chevron) but share the same typographic weight and tone. The result is a family of brands that are instantly recognisable as connected without being identical.

The Logos

Each brand has its own colour identity while sharing a unified typographic system. The "event" / "hire" split typography ties the core brands together, with sector names and individual colour palettes differentiating each company.

What I Learned

A rebrand isn't just logos and colours — it's a strategic exercise in understanding how a business wants to be perceived. The hardest part wasn't the design work; it was getting stakeholder alignment across multiple brands with different priorities. Each company had its own ideas about how it should look, and the challenge was creating a system flexible enough to accommodate those differences while still achieving the cohesion the group needed.

Working across print, digital, signage, and vehicle livery also reinforced that a brand identity needs to work at every scale and on every medium — what looks great on a screen might not work on a van wrap.
